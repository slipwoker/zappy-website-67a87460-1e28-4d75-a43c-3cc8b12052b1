  How does the process for a new mortgage begin? : 
 To obtain a mortgage there are several critical criteria
Criterion #1 - Financing percentage
The financing percentage is the ratio between the property value and the maximum loan amount the bank can approve.
Important emphasis: The maximum mortgage amount is derived from the property value according to the purchase contract or alternatively the appraiser's valuation - whichever is lower.
Additional emphasis: Transaction-related costs are not included in the property value.
Single apartment - You can get up to 75% financing of property value. That is, if the property value is ₪1,000,000, the maximum mortgage you can get is ₪750,000. The difference + related costs will need to be brought as equity from home.
Investment apartment - Defined as a second apartment and more. If I own a property with or without a mortgage and want to purchase an additional property, the maximum financing available will be up to 50% of the purchased property value.
Leveraging existing property - Do we own an existing property? You can get a mortgage loan on an existing property up to 50% of the existing property value (subject to appraiser evaluation, of course). If existing property value is ₪1,000,000, you can get up to ₪500,000 mortgage. If there is a mortgage on the property, you can get the difference up to ₪500,000.
Criterion #2 - Borrowers' age
You can get a mortgage until age 75 or for a maximum period of 30 years. That is, if we are 45 or younger, we can get the loan for up to 30 years. If our age is 55, we can get the loan for a maximum period of 20 years.
Criterion 3 for obtaining a mortgage - Conduct / credit data
Proper credit data in the present and past refers to our level of conduct in various banks. As long as our conduct is proper, meaning we meet all our obligations, from the bank's perspective everything is fine.
Beyond the bank requiring to see the last 3 months of salary slips, it examines credit history by ID number of each borrower.

Bankruptcy Proper conduct is a necessary condition for obtaining a mortgage. Today anyone can examine credit data through the Bank of Israel credit data system.
Criterion 4 - Property integrity at legal and planning levels
What is an improper property?

Debts on property / garnishments Fifth criterion - repayment ratio
The repayment ratio from income is the maximum monthly repayment the bank can approve for us and this is one of the data points that limits us in terms of the mortgage amount we can take.

 All-purpose mortgage  : 
  An all-purpose mortgage loan is a loan given against collateral of an existing property. There are two types of mortgage loans: Mortgage loan for housing purposes All-purpose mortgage loan In both cases the property serves as collateral to the bank for the loan. The difference between the loans is in the cost of money - the interest rate. In mortgage loans for housing the interest rate will be lower. A mortgage loan for housing is a loan given for purchasing an additional property and an all-purpose mortgage loan is a loan given against an existing property for any purpose that is not purchasing an existing property. What is an all-purpose mortgage loan? An all-purpose mortgage loan is essentially a loan given against collateral of an existing property, when the purpose of the loan is not for purchasing a property or expanding the property against building permits. An all-purpose mortgage loan can be for property renovation, closing existing loans and additional obligations, purchasing a car, furniture, etc... An all-purpose loan can be obtained up to 50% of property value. That is, if we own a property worth ₪1,000,000 we can get an all-purpose loan up to ₪500,000 minus the existing mortgage. That is, if we own a property worth ₪1,000,000 with a mortgage of ₪350,000, we can get an all-purpose loan of ₪150,000. That is - up to 50% financing of property value. Just as you need to approve obtaining a mortgage loan, the same applies to an all-purpose loan against property, you need to obtain preliminary approval for the loan. What are the criteria for obtaining an all-purpose mortgage loan: Proper checking account conduct for 3 months Proper conduct in mortgage loan for the last two years if there is an existing mortgage Proper credit history Proper financing percentage Proper repayment ratio - income high enough relative to required loan All-purpose mortgage - when to take and what are the advantages: Consolidating existing loans for long term to reduce monthly payments Easier cash flow Reducing obligation (credit) in bank account Chance for new loan at cheaper interest rate Loan for various purposes in relatively high amounts. Examples of data-driven decision making

Young couple in Tel Aviv – balancing interest rates and flexibility

A couple in their 30s purchased a first apartment for 1.8 million ₪. They were torn between a fixed interest rate track for 20 years (3.2%) and a combination of fixed and prime. After a detailed comparison of future interest rate scenarios and assessment of monthly repayment capacity, a combination of 60% fixed and 40% prime was recommended. The approach focused on reducing exposure to interest rate increases while maintaining flexibility for early repayment in case of income changes.

Family from central Israel – mortgage refinancing and loan consolidation

A family with an existing mortgage (outstanding debt of 900,000 ₪) and consumer debts (150,000 ₪) sought consultation. The analysis compared mortgage refinancing with new tracks versus loan consolidation. After examining early repayment fees, different bank terms, and future cash flow, it was decided to refinance the mortgage with debt consolidation in a combined track. The process focused on reducing the monthly payment by 2,800 ₪ while maintaining a reasonable time horizon.
